How this works 0:10–0:12 · Our agreements

This is a cohort, not a class

You'll hear from each other every single week — that's by design. A few agreements to make this room safe:

  • Stories stay, lessons leave. What's shared here is confidential.
  • Every path is welcome. Bedside, clinic, leadership, already searching, just curious — nobody is behind.
  • Cameras on when you can — and audio-only is always okay.
  • Discussion over lecture. The chat is always open; your experience is course material.
  • No one will tell you to quit — or to stay. You'll decide, informed.

The Career Direction Method 0:23–0:27 · The big idea

Three parts. Three questions.

1

Name the Strain

What is wearing you down?

Score your body load, interruption load, and change load from 1–10. Strain isn't weakness — it's information.

2

Capture Your Value

What do you bring with you?

Name the strengths you keep dismissing because they feel ordinary. AI cannot duplicate them.

3

Investigate Your Options

What's worth investigating next?

Reconnaissance turns fear into information: study the work, ask people who know it, compare what you learn.

Not one decision made in a panic — a method you can learn, repeat, and walk with a cohort beside you.

The reframe 0:27–0:32 · Discussion

“Your highest score doesn't tell you to panic.
It tells you where to look first.”

Strain arrives in three forms — and you can name yours:

  • Body load — lifting, standing, transferring, needing longer to recover
  • Interruption load — alarms, call lights, open mental loops, constant task-switching
  • Change load — new systems and expectations, learned at full speed
💬
In the chat

Which one do you feel most right now? Type B (body), I (interruption), or C (change).

Learning goals 0:32–0:35 · What you'll leave with

By Week 6, you will be able to…

  • Name what's wearing you down — your three loads, scored, in writing
  • Describe five strengths you'd bring to a next role — without shame
  • Investigate a role before applying — five postings, three conversations, one comparison
  • Know your money questions — the income, benefit, and timing facts to gather
  • Commit to one informed step — specific, chosen by you, with a date

And we measure it together: a short survey this week, the same one at the end. You'll see your own change.
